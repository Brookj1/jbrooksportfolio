About Me

After choosing to believe that you have the power to affect positive change under any circumstances, I dove headfirst into higher education as a full-time student while also a full-time employee and single parent. During this experience I began to understand how I could use my varied background and creativity to solve problems that could positively affect strategic initiatives. Using this newfound knowledge I earned a BS in Organizational Leadership with a minor in Communications from Colorado State University. Shortly thereafter, my professional path led me to a software start-up where I was immediately enamored with development. Inspired, I signed up for the University of Denver’s first all women Full Stack Development Bootcamp. This has resulted in a happy union of my love of learning, creativity, propensity for organization, and my abilty to think strategically. My goal is to continue coding, building, creating, and growing.

Digital Bibliophile

Project

Repository

Digital Bibliophile

Group project created with three of my bootcamp classmates. Beta version of app (MVP) that allows users to search for digital books and check their availability using the Digital Public Library of America and Open Library APIs. Future iterations would include integrations with OverDrive and Google Maps to allow users to search their local libraries for books and link to their sites for quick and painless digital checkout. For this project we all worked on every aspect of the app, including HTML, CSS, Materialize, JavaScript/jQuery, APIs, Ajax, and JSON. We used GitHub for collaboration and version control, and Trello and Slack for project management.
